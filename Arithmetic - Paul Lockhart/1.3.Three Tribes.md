# The deepest idea in this chapter

Most people think:

> Arithmetic = doing calculations.

Lockhart says:

> Arithmetic = organizing information.

If you can take

* claps
* thumps
* rocks
* bananas
* trees
* symbols

and convert one representation into another,

then you're already doing arithmetic.

Addition, subtraction, multiplication, place value—all the later chapters are just more advanced versions of this same activity: **taking quantities and reorganizing them into more useful groups.** 🚀📖
