# Final Message of the Chapter

The entire chapter can be summarized in one sentence:

> **Addition made all number systems roughly equal. Multiplication is where place-value systems become overwhelmingly powerful.**

And the deepest insight is:

> **School multiplication is not a mysterious algorithm.**
>
> It is simply:
>
> 1. Break numbers into place values.
> 2. Multiply every piece with every other piece.
> 3. Add the results.
>
> That's it.

This is why Lockhart says multiplication is really about **rectangles, decomposition, and place shifting**, not about memorizing tables or blindly following rules.
