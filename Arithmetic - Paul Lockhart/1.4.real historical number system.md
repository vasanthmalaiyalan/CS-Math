# Chapter Summary

Egyptian arithmetic can be reduced to 3 rules:

### Rule 1

Every symbol has a fixed value.

```text
1 = 1
g = 10
p = 100
X = 1000
```

---

### Rule 2

To add numbers,

just combine all symbols together.

---

### Rule 3

Whenever you get 10 identical symbols,

exchange them for the next larger symbol.

```text
10 ones → 1 ten
10 tens → 1 hundred
10 hundreds → 1 thousand
```

---

And that's the big realization Lockhart wants you to have:

> **Addition is not a mysterious procedure.**
>
> It is simply collecting symbols and exchanging groups for larger groups.

That's exactly the same idea behind our modern decimal system. Egyptians just made the process visible with physical symbols and counting coins. 🔥📖

The next big step in the book is when he starts showing why our modern place-value system (the system using 0–9) is much more powerful than Egyptian notation. That's where the story gets really interesting. 🚀
